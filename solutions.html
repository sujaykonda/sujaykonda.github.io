Solutions & advocacy

What to change—on campus and beyond

Sportsbooks profit when their heaviest users lose often. Meaningful protection has to come from policy, not just “self-control.”

Campus actions Policy levers

Goal

Make betting harder to start, easier to quit, and impossible to mistake for “free money.”
Policy

Federal and state regulators

Restrict ads near campuses

Ban “risk-free” language, boost font sizes on odds/conditions, and prohibit promo boards at college venues.

Close under-21 loopholes

Stop platforms from targeting 18–20-year-olds online; require age verification that isn’t bypassed by cash apps or prepaid cards.

Mandate safer defaults

Hard deposit caps for new users, cooling-off periods after losses, and a ban on “instant” deposits without a short delay.

Campus

Changes universities can make now

Add gambling literacy to orientation

Ten-minute module on odds, inducements, and warning signs.

Pause sponsorships

Reject sportsbook ads and in-stadium promos near student sections; replace with better sponsorships.

Set platform requirements

If any app is campus-promoted, require default deposit caps, and cooling-off periods.

Talking points

How to push back on industry arguments

“We boost the economy.”

Those dollars come from people losing. Is it really worth profitting from student losses?

“Regulation already protects users.”

As long as profit depends on heavy losses, self-regulation will be weak. Protection and profit are misaligned.

“It’s a personal choice.”

Choice assumes honest information. Ads that label bets “free money” distort risk.

Pair these steps with the Stories page to ground policy in lived experience.
