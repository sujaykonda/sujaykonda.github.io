Audience & aim

Who this is for

I built this site for two overlapping audiences: students who see betting as “just part of sports,” and campus leaders who can change the environment. Instead of academic prose, this remix uses stories, visuals, and quick actions to make the risks obvious and the solutions doable.

Students

Spot manipulative tactics, recognize social pressure, and know where to get help before debt or isolation sets in.

Campus leaders

Policy levers, messaging, and programming you can implement this semester: ad limits, orientation content, and real support.

Anyone skeptical

Common rebuttals answered: “It’s just entertainment,” “Regulation already protects people,” and “It’s their choice.”

Actions you can take

Make change this semester

Close the ad firehose

Email campus marketing/athletics about pausing sportsbook sponsorships and prohibiting “risk-free” promo boards near games.

Add gambling literacy to orientation

Push for a 10-minute module on odds, inducements, and warning signs. Bias toward visuals and quick self-checks over policy jargon.

Reclaim the group chat

Share the Stories page when friends call a bet “free money.” Normalize opting out—and invite people to game nights that aren’t wagers.

Build real guardrails

Ask student government to advocate for default deposit caps and cooling-off periods on any campus-promoted apps.
